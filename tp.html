Couscous

Couscous is a type of pasta made from crushed durum wheat semolina, originating from North Africa. It has a light, fluffy texture and a slightly nutty flavor. Traditionally, couscous is steamed and served as a base for various dishes such as stews, tagines, or salads. It's a versatile ingredient that can be flavored with herbs, spices, and vegetables.

Pastilla

Pastilla: Traditional Moroccan dish consisting of thin layers of pastry filled with spiced meat (often pigeon or chicken), almonds, and eggs, all dusted with powdered sugar and cinnamon. Delicate, savory-sweet flavor profile with a crisp, flaky texture. Enjoyed as a special occasion dish during celebrations and festivals

Tanjia

Tanjia: A slow-cooked meat dish originating from Marrakech, Morocco, prepared in a clay pot called a "tanjia." Typically made with lamb, seasoned with a blend of spices including cumin, saffron, and garlic, then cooked for several hours in a wood-fired oven or over low heat. Results in tender, flavorful meat with a rich and aromatic sauce.
